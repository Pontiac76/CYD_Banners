OneROM is an open-source ROM
replacement for retro computers.
It uses a very low-cost microcontroller
and can emulate many classic ROM chips.

It supports 24, 28, 32, and 40 pin
EPROMs and mask-programmed ROMs used
in many computer-based systems from
the late 1970s through early 1990s.
